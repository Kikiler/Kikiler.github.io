About Us

From a study-hall sketch to the starting grid.

It started with a realization.

Early in the year, a few of us noticed something strange: all the school’s automotive clubs and projects had quietly disappeared. They no longer fit the administration’s vision of becoming a more sustainable, environmentally responsible school. At first, it felt like the end of something — like there was no place left for students who loved building, designing, and working with machines.

But then someone found it: a competition happening not far from us, where only electric cars were allowed to race. That changed everything.

Instead of seeing sustainability as a limitation, we started seeing it as a direction. What if we built something that aligned with that vision instead of fighting it?

The idea spread slowly at first. A few conversations after class. A couple of rough sketches. Then we began talking to teachers — not with a finished plan, but with an ambition. Surprisingly, they listened. Even more surprisingly, they supported us. What began as a small group grew, step by step. Each new person brought something different: design ideas, technical skills, organization, motivation. We weren’t just trying to build a car anymore — we were building a project that made sense in today’s world. Now, we’re fully committed.

Our goal isn’t just to participate in a competition. It’s bigger than that. We want to become the first French-speaking school to lead a truly sustainable technical project — one that proves engineering, innovation, and environmental responsibility can go hand in hand.

We're not here to play. We're here to race.
— The ESET crew
