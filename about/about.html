About Us

From a study-hall sketch to the starting grid.

It started with a napkin. October, junior year — Maya Chen and Diego Alvarez were supposed to be revising for a chemistry test. Instead, Diego sketched a roll cage on the back of a worksheet and slid it across the table. "What if we actually did it?"

Two weeks later they cornered Mr. Holloway, the shop teacher, and pitched him a deal: give them a corner of the garage and they'd turn the school's forgotten 1995 Mazda Miata — donated years ago and slowly rusting behind the gym — into a real, logbooked race car.

Mr. Holloway said yes on one condition: they had to teach the next generation everything they learned. By December the team had grown to seven. By February, fifteen. Sam handled suspension geometry between calc problems. Priya rewired the entire harness from scratch. Jordan, the youngest at fourteen, turned out to be the best welder in the building.

The car fired for the first time on a freezing Tuesday in March. The whole shop class poured out of the building when they heard it. We have a long road to the first green flag — seat fitting, scrutineering, raising the entry fee through bake sales and merch — but for the first time, it feels real.

We're not here to play. We're here to race.
— The ESET crew
